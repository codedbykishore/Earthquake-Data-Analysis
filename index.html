QuakeVision

Your window into the Earth's seismic activity — track earthquakes as they happen, explore historical data, and predict potential risks with advanced analytics.

QuakeVision combines real-time earthquake monitoring, machine learning predictions, and interactive visualizations to help researchers, policymakers, and enthusiasts understand the dynamics of seismic events globally.

Dataset Sources

USGS Earthquake Catalog

The United States Geological Survey provides real-time earthquake data worldwide, including magnitudes, locations, depths, and timestamps. Ideal for monitoring seismic activity and historical trend analysis.

Visit USGS

Global Seismic Network (GSN)

A worldwide network of seismometers maintained by IRIS, offering precise measurements of earthquakes and other seismic events. GSN data is essential for academic research and earthquake modeling.

Explore GSN

EM-DAT: The International Disaster Database

EM-DAT compiles historical records of natural and technological disasters globally. It provides statistics on human impact, economic losses, and disaster frequency—perfect for risk assessment and planning.

Visit EM-DAT
